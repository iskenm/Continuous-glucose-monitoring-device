# User Needs

Document ID: UNS-CGM3000-001
Revision: 2.3
Effective Date: 2025-06-15

## UN-001: Continuous Glucose Monitoring

Users need to continuously monitor their glucose levels without frequent fingerstick blood tests so they can better manage their diabetes.

**Stakeholders**: Patients, Endocrinologists
**Priority**: Essential

## UN-002: Real-Time Glucose Display

Users need to see their current glucose level and trend direction on their mobile device at any time.

**Stakeholders**: Patients
**Priority**: Essential

## UN-003: Hypoglycemia Alerts

Users need to be warned when their glucose level drops to a dangerously low level so they can take corrective action before a severe hypoglycemic event.

**Stakeholders**: Patients, Caregivers
**Priority**: Essential

## UN-004: Hyperglycemia Alerts

Users need to be notified when their glucose level rises above their target range so they can administer insulin or take other corrective measures.

**Stakeholders**: Patients, Endocrinologists
**Priority**: Essential

## UN-005: Trend Information

Users need to understand whether their glucose is rising, falling, or stable so they can anticipate future glucose levels and plan meals and insulin doses.

**Stakeholders**: Patients
**Priority**: Essential

## UN-006: Historical Data Review

Users need to review their glucose data over hours, days, and weeks to identify patterns and share information with their healthcare provider.

**Stakeholders**: Patients, Endocrinologists
**Priority**: Important

## UN-007: Minimal Calibration

Users want a device that requires minimal or no fingerstick calibration to reduce the burden of device maintenance.

**Stakeholders**: Patients
**Priority**: Important

## UN-008: Extended Sensor Wear

Users want a sensor that can be worn for an extended period (at least 10 days) to minimize the frequency of sensor insertion.

**Stakeholders**: Patients
**Priority**: Important

## UN-009: Comfortable Wear

Users need the device to be small, lightweight, and comfortable enough for continuous 24/7 wear including during sleep and physical activity.

**Stakeholders**: Patients
**Priority**: Important

## UN-010: Water Resistance

Users need to wear the device during showering and light water activities without damaging the device or affecting accuracy.

**Stakeholders**: Patients
**Priority**: Important

## UN-011: Data Sharing

Users want to share their glucose data with caregivers and family members for remote monitoring, especially for elderly patients or children transitioning to adult care.

**Stakeholders**: Patients, Caregivers
**Priority**: Desirable

## UN-012: Discreet Operation

Users want the device to operate discreetly without drawing attention, particularly in social and professional settings.

**Stakeholders**: Patients
**Priority**: Desirable
